House of the Immortal Poet

The Soul of Usiacurí

Experience the home where Colombia's greatest Romantic poet, Julio Flórez, found his final inspiration. A sanctuary of literature, melancholy, and natural beauty.

Heritage

A Poet's Refuge

Julio Flórez arrived in Usiacurí in 1909, seeking the healing properties of the medicinal waters. What began as a health retreat became his permanent home, where he lived for 14 years and created his most profound late works.

"Flores Negras"

"Oye: bajo las ruinas de mi aliento, hay un mundo de sombras y de luces..." Relive the melancholic verses that made him the voice of a generation in the early 20th century.

National Monument

Declared a National Cultural Interest Site in 2007, the museum preserves the poet's original tomb, his library, and personal furniture.

The Eternal Collection

Original Manuscripts

View the original ink pages where the poet expressed his deep romanticism and his love for the Colombian landscape.

Personal Gallery

A collection of rare photographs documenting his life in Usiacurí alongside his wife, Petrona Moreno, and their children.
